Implemented a Silver Layer data quality framework in Databricks using PySpark, including null validation, duplicate removal, referential integrity checks, rejected-record handling, and audit metrics generation within a Medallion Architecture.

# Day 05 - Silver Layer & Data Quality Framework

## Objective

Convert raw Bronze data into clean and validated Silver data using data quality checks and business rules.

Flow:

CSV Files
↓
Bronze Tables
↓
Data Quality Checks
↓
Silver Tables
↓
Rejected Tables

---

## Medallion Architecture

### Bronze Layer

* Stores raw source data
* No transformations
* Source of truth

### Silver Layer

* Cleans and validates data
* Applies business rules
* Removes bad records

### Gold Layer

* Business KPIs
* Reporting
* Analytics

---

## Data Quality Checks Implemented

### 1. Null Validation

Purpose:

* Identify mandatory fields with NULL values

Example:
customer_name cannot be NULL

---

### 2. Duplicate Removal

Purpose:

* Remove duplicate business records

Method:
dropDuplicates(["customer_id"])

---

### 3. Referential Integrity Validation

Purpose:

* Ensure relationships between tables are valid

Examples:

* Account must belong to a valid Customer
* Loan must belong to a valid Customer
* Transaction must belong to a valid Account

---

### 4. Rejected Records Framework

Purpose:

* Store invalid records separately instead of deleting them

Tables Created:

* rejected_customers
* rejected_accounts
* rejected_loans
* rejected_transactions

Benefits:

* Auditing
* Troubleshooting
* Data Governance

---

### 5. Data Quality Metrics

Metrics Captured:

* Total Records
* Valid Records
* Rejected Records

Formula:

Total Records = Valid Records + Rejected Records

Purpose:

* Monitor pipeline health
* Track data quality

---

## Important PySpark Concepts

### Inner Join

Returns matching records from both tables.

Use Case:
Valid Accounts and Valid Transactions.

---

### Left Anti Join

Returns records from left table that do not have matching records in right table.

Use Case:
Find invalid Accounts, Loans, and Transactions.

---

### dropDuplicates()

Removes duplicate records using business keys.

---

### filter()

Used for validation checks.

Examples:

* NULL checks
* Negative amount checks

---

## Banking Business Rules Implemented

### Customers

* customer_name cannot be NULL

### Accounts

* customer_id must exist in Customers table

### Loans

* loan_amount > 0
* customer_id must exist

### Transactions

* amount > 0
* account_id must exist
* transaction_id should be unique

## Interview Questions

Q. What is the purpose of the Silver Layer?

Answer:
The Silver Layer cleans, validates, and standardizes raw data before it is used for analytics and reporting.

---

Q. Why use Left Anti Join?

Answer:
Left Anti Join is used to identify records that do not have matching records in another table, helping enforce referential integrity.

---

Q. Why maintain Rejected Tables?

Answer:
Rejected tables preserve invalid records for auditing, troubleshooting, and data governance instead of permanently deleting them.

---

Q. How did you monitor data quality?

Answer:
Created an audit table containing total, valid, and rejected records for each dataset after validation.

---

## Key Learning

A production-ready ETL pipeline should not only load data but also validate, clean, audit, and track rejected records before making data available for analytics.
